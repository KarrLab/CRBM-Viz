runBioSimulations is a free tool for executing a wide range of simulations. runBioSimulations provides the same simulation capabilities as BioSimulations via a simpler interface that doesn't require an account. runBioSimulations supports a broad range of frameworks (e.g., logical, kinetic), simulation algorithms (e.g., FBA, SSA), formats (e.g., SBML, SED-ML), and simulation tools (e.g., COBRApy, COPASI). Helping investigators execute biomodeling studies runBioSimulations provides a central place for executing simulations of a broad range of modeling frameworks, simulation algorithms, model formats, and simulation tools. runBioSimulations provides a simple web interface for interactively plotting the results of simulations as time series charts. By making it easier to execute simulations, runBioSimulations makes it easier for investigators to reuse published simulations and for peer reviewers to evaluate simulations. Central access to multiple modeling frameworks, algorithms, formats & tools

About runBioSimulations

runBioSimulations was developed by the Center for Reproducible Biomedical Modeling , the Karr Lab at the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai , and the Moraru Lab at the University of Connecticut Health Center with support from the National Institutes of Health and the National Science Foundation.

runBioSimulations is implemented using several open-source tools and cloud platforms. The models, simulations, and visualizations are available under the licenses specified for each resource. The code is openly available under the MIT license .
